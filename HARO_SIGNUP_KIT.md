### Worked example — query: *"What's a realistic 2026 401(k) contribution strategy for someone earning $90K?"*

> Hi Sarah,
>
> Quick context on me: I'm Jahanzeb Nawaz, founder of FinBrief (finbrief.space). I cover retirement contribution strategy in depth.
>
> For 2026, the 401(k) elective deferral limit is **$24,500** (IRS IR-2025-111, Nov 13, 2025). A $90K earner should target the full employer match first (typically a 3–6% contribution unlocks the full match), then layer in Roth IRA contributions up to the $7,500 2026 limit. If there's room left, return to the 401(k). The reason for sequencing IRA before maxing 401(k) is the broader investment menu and lower fees a self-directed IRA usually offers vs. a workplace plan.
>
> Happy to be quoted as: "Jahanzeb Nawaz, founder of FinBrief (finbrief.space)."
>
> Best,
> Jahanzeb

- **5 responses/week** is the playbook target. At ~5% pickup, that's roughly 1 inbound link per month.
- **Respond within 2 hours** of the query email — most queries close in 24h.
- **Track every response** in a one-row-per-pitch log (slug below).
